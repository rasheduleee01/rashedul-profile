Thesis: Design and Analysis of a Cost Effective Solar PV System

Abstract

Sun is the origin of all sources. Solar energy is a renewable, efficient, and environmentally friendly energy source for industrial, commercial, and residential applications. This work discusses solar panels and associated accessories like solar array mounts and inverter systems to design a solar PV system. Considering 30% of the rooftop area, the daily output from the designed system is 477.22 KWh. The performance of the system was simulated using PVSYST software, resulting in an annual performance ratio of 82.37%. One key focus of the thesis is selecting efficient solar modules at a reasonable cost.

Introduction

Solar energy refers to solar radiation energy and can be utilized via photo-thermal conversion, photovoltaic, or photochemical conversion. Solar power is a primary source for other energy types like wind, water, and chemical energy. Main uses include solar cells, solar water heaters, and hot water generation. Solar energy is clean, abundant, and increasingly critical as society seeks new, sustainable energy sources. The photovoltaic (PV) effect was first observed in 1839, and since the 1980s, solar PV generation has grown rapidly, particularly in Japan, the EU, and the United States.

Motivation

Human society faces energy depletion and environmental pollution. Non-renewable resources are diminishing, and solar power provides a free, abundant, and environmentally friendly energy source. This research focuses on utilizing solar power efficiently for electricity generation, combining PV cells and panels to design cost-effective systems accessible to common people.

Research Objectives

  • Study photovoltaic (PV) solar power generation systems.
  • Analyze solar insolation and irradiance for the selected location.
  • Design a PV solar system for a selected load.
  • Simulate the designed PV system using PVSYST software.
  • Determine a cost-effective system through approximate cost calculations.

Research Methodology

Solar insolation and daily sunlight data were collected for the selected location to calculate energy output. The PV system was designed for the estimated load and simulated using PVSYST software to verify performance. Finally, the approximate system cost was calculated to ensure cost-effectiveness.

Thesis Organization

The thesis is organized into four chapters: Chapter 1 covers PV cell overview, concepts, modeling, classification, and maintenance; Chapter 2 describes PV system composition; Chapter 3 discusses PV system selection; Chapter 4 presents system design, simulation, and approximate cost analysis.

Conclusion

The horizontal global irradiation is 474.5 W/m² for an average solar insolation of 4.65 kWh/m². Theoretically calculated annual energy is 193.54 MWh/year, while PVSYST simulation produced 184 MWh/year with a performance ratio of 82.37%. The cost of generated electricity is approximately 5.78 Taka per KWh, compared to 9.8 Taka/KWh from the grid, demonstrating that the designed system is cost-effective.

Future Work

Future improvements include optimizing PV array tilt for maximum power output, implementing automatic solar tracking systems, and integrating PLC and SCADA systems to enhance solar energy generation efficiency.
